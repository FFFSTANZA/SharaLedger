Sales & Customer Success

Master the art of revenue management with Versoll Books. From tracking your customers to creating professional invoices and collecting payments‚Äîthis comprehensive guide covers everything you need to keep your sales cycle running smoothly.

What you'll learn: How to manage customer relationships, create GST-compliant invoices, and efficiently track payments to keep your cash flow healthy.

Part 1: Managing Your Customer Directory

Your customers are the heart of your business. Versoll Books makes it simple to organize and track every customer relationship, from small one-time buyers to major recurring clients.

1

Creating a New Customer

Navigate to Customers > New Customer to add a customer to your directory. Essential fields include:

  • Customer Name: The legal or business name of your customer.
  • Email: For sending invoices and payment reminders.
  • Phone: Primary contact number.
  • Billing Address: Where invoices should be sent.

Pro Tip: Use a consistent naming format (e.g., "Company Name - Location") to easily search and identify customers when creating invoices.

2

GST & Tax Configuration

For Indian businesses, accurate GST tracking is critical. When creating or editing a customer, specify:

  • GSTIN: The customer's 15-digit GST Identification Number (e.g., 27AAAPL1234C1Z5).
  • State: Customer's state for inter-state vs intra-state GST calculation.
  • Customer Type: Registered (has GSTIN) or Unregistered.

Important: If the customer's state differs from your company state, Versoll Books will automatically apply IGST instead of CGST+SGST. Ensure the state is set correctly!

3

Credit Terms & Payment Preferences

Define how and when you expect to be paid:

  • Credit Days: Payment due period (e.g., Net 30, Net 15).
  • Credit Limit: Maximum outstanding amount allowed for the customer.
  • Default Payment Method: Cash, Bank Transfer, Cheque, UPI, etc.

Versoll Books will automatically calculate due dates based on credit terms and alert you when credit limits are exceeded.

Part 2: Creating Professional Invoices

Invoicing is where your hard work turns into revenue. Versoll Books ensures every invoice is accurate, compliant, and professional.

4

Starting a New Sales Invoice

Go to Sales > New Sales Invoice. The invoice form includes:

  • Customer: Select from your customer directory (or create new).
  • Invoice Number: Auto-generated, but you can customize the format in Settings.
  • Invoice Date: Transaction date (defaults to today).
  • Due Date: Automatically calculated based on customer's credit terms.
5

Adding Items and Services

In the Items section, add the products or services you're selling:

  • Item Name: Select from your Item Master or create new.
  • Quantity: Number of units sold.
  • Rate: Price per unit.
  • Discount: Optional discount (percentage or fixed amount).
  • Tax Rate: GST rate will auto-fill based on item configuration (5%, 12%, 18%, 28%).

Versoll Books automatically calculates:

  • Subtotal (Quantity √ó Rate - Discount)
  • GST breakdown (CGST+SGST for intra-state, IGST for inter-state)
  • Grand Total (Subtotal + Taxes)
6

Invoice Customization & Notes

Make your invoices more informative with additional details:

  • Terms & Conditions: Payment terms, return policies, warranty info.
  • Notes: Special instructions or thank you messages.
  • Reference Number: PO number or project code for customer's tracking.

Tip: Set default terms & conditions in Settings > Invoice Defaults to save time on every invoice.

7

Saving and Submitting Invoices

Versoll Books uses a two-stage invoice lifecycle:

  • Save as Draft: Invoice is editable but not recorded in books. Use this while you're still finalizing details.
  • Submit: Locks the invoice and records it in your accounting ledger. This creates proper GL entries (Debit Receivables, Credit Sales, Credit GST Payable).

Important: Once submitted, an invoice cannot be edited. You can only cancel it and create a Credit Note if corrections are needed.

Printing and Sharing Invoices

After submitting an invoice, you can:

  • Print: Generate a professional PDF for physical delivery.
  • Email: Send directly to customer's registered email.
  • Export: Save as PDF for your records or customer portals.

Part 3: Recording Payments & Managing Collections

Creating invoices is only half the job‚Äîcollecting payments is where cash flow happens. Versoll Books makes payment tracking simple and transparent.

8

Creating a Payment Entry

When a customer pays, record it immediately. Navigate to Sales > Payment Entry or click Receive Payment from the invoice view. Fill in:

  • Customer: Who is making the payment.
  • Payment Date: When payment was received.
  • Payment Method: Cash, Bank Transfer, Cheque, UPI, Credit Card.
  • Amount: Total payment received.
  • Received In: Select the bank account or cash account where money is deposited.
9

Allocating Payments to Invoices

In the Invoice Allocation section, Versoll Books shows all outstanding invoices for the selected customer:

  • Full Payment: Check the invoice to allocate the full outstanding amount.
  • Partial Payment: Enter a custom amount if the customer is paying in installments.
  • Multiple Invoices: Allocate a single payment across multiple invoices.

Versoll Books will show remaining amounts and automatically mark invoices as "Paid" when fully settled.

10

Handling Advances & Credits

Sometimes customers pay before the invoice is created (advance payments) or overpay:

  • Advance Payment: Create a Payment Entry without allocating to any invoice. Versoll Books tracks this as a customer credit.
  • Using Advances: When creating future invoices for that customer, apply the advance credit to reduce the payable amount.
  • Refunds: If you need to return money, create a Payment Entry with negative amount or use the Refund option.

Tracking Outstanding Payments

To monitor who owes you money and when, use these powerful reports:

üìä Accounts Receivable Aging

Navigate to Reports > Accounts Receivable Aging to see outstanding invoices grouped by age:

  • Current: Not yet due
  • 1-30 Days: Recently overdue
  • 31-60 Days: Requires follow-up
  • 60+ Days: Critical collection needed

This report helps you prioritize collection efforts and identify problematic customers.

üë§ Customer Ledger

View a complete transaction history with any customer by going to Reports > General Ledger and filtering by customer account. This shows:

  • All invoices (increases balance)
  • All payments received (decreases balance)
  • Credit notes and adjustments
  • Running balance after each transaction

Payment Collection Best Practices

  • Record Promptly: Enter payments as soon as they're received to keep your cash flow reports accurate.
  • Set Reminders: Use credit terms to automatically calculate due dates, then review your Aging report weekly.
  • Reconcile Regularly: Match payment entries with your bank statements using the Banking module (covered in the next tutorial).
  • Follow Up Early: Contact customers 5-7 days before payment is due to prevent late payments.
