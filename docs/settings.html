Settings & Configuration

Use Settings to define company information, fiscal rules, user access, and system defaults. These configurations keep workflows consistent across your organization.

What you will configure

Company profile, fiscal year, tax settings, permissions, notifications, and integrations.

Company Profile

Set up the legal and operational details that appear on invoices and reports.

Step 1

Update Company Information

Go to Settings > Company and enter legal name, address, GSTIN, PAN, and contact details.

Step 2

Define Fiscal Year

Select the fiscal year start month and set the current financial year. This governs GST reporting and year-end closing.

Users and Permissions

Assign access based on roles such as Accountant, Sales, or Inventory Manager.

Step 3

Create Roles

Use the role builder to define allowed modules, reports, and approval limits.

Step 4

Invite Users

Add team members with their email IDs and assign them to roles. Each user gets a login with audit trails.

Tax and Compliance Defaults

Set defaults for GST rates, TDS sections, and invoice numbering so transactions stay compliant.

  • Configure GST registration type and default state.
  • Define TDS sections used most frequently.
  • Set invoice numbering series per document type.

Banking and Payment Settings

  • Map default bank accounts for receipts and payments.
  • Enable UPI and payment links on sales invoices.
  • Set approval thresholds for large payments.

Notifications and Alerts

Manage automatic reminders and alerts for overdue invoices, low stock, and compliance deadlines.

Step 5

Configure Notification Rules

Select which events trigger emails or in-app alerts. Use templates to standardize the messaging.

Step 6

Set Approval Workflows

Define approval paths for purchases, expenses, and stock adjustments.

Integrations

Connect external services such as payroll, CRM tools, or payment gateways for streamlined operations.

  • Enable API access keys and manage tokens.
  • Schedule data syncs with third-party apps.
  • Review logs for integration health.

Review settings quarterly to keep tax rates, contact details, and approval workflows up to date.
