Chart of Accounts

The Chart of Accounts (CoA) is the backbone of your accounting system. It is a categorized list of all accounts where your business transactions are recorded.

Understanding the Hierarchy

Versoll Books uses a standard 5-category system to keep your financials organized. Every account falls under one of these primary groups:

  • Assets: What your business owns (Cash, Inventory, Equipment).
  • Liabilities: What your business owes (Loans, GST Payable, TDS Payable).
  • Equity: The owner's stake in the business.
  • Income: Revenue generated from sales and services.
  • Expenses: Costs incurred to run the business (Rent, Salaries).

Key Accounts in Versoll Books

While you can create custom accounts, Versoll Books manages several critical accounts automatically to ensure compliance:

Tax Accounts

When you enable GST or TDS, Versoll Books automatically tracks these in dedicated liability accounts:

  • GST Payable: Tracks Output GST collected from customers.
  • ITC (Input Tax Credit): Tracks GST paid to suppliers that you can claim back.
  • TDS Payable: Tracks Tax Deducted at Source that needs to be paid to the government.

Bank and Cash

Manage your liquid assets by creating separate accounts for each bank account and cash drawer. This is essential for the Banking & Reconciliation workflow.

Pro Tip: Use a clear naming convention for your accounts (e.g., "HDFC Bank - Main Account") to make transaction entry faster and reports easier to read.

Customizing your CoA

To add or modify accounts, navigate to Accounting > Chart of Accounts. You can create sub-accounts to track expenses in more detail (e.g., creating "Office Rent" and "Warehouse Rent" under a parent "Rent" expense).
